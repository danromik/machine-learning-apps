# 3×3 Cube — 5-Minute Training Demo: Final Report

**Run:** `20260626-223041` · ~5 min on MPS · 1500 value-iteration iterations
**Algorithm:** value iteration over a learned cost-to-go (DeepCubeA-lite), ~298K params
**Curriculum:** reverse-scramble, k=1 → reached k=11, promote at 90% solve-rate
**Final checkpoint:** `autorun_20260626-223041.pt`

**A genuinely solid solver.** Any cube scrambled **up to 8 moves** gets solved
essentially every time, 9–10 moves nearly always, and even 11-move scrambles ~3
times in 4 — in five minutes of training, from random weights.

## What this taught
- **The fix for sparse reward works.** A scrambled 3×3 has one goal among
  ~4.3×10¹⁹ states; blind model-free RL would never find it. We never relied on
  stumbling onto reward — we learned a **cost-to-go** (moves-to-solve) by
  bootstrapping each state's value from its children, then used **beam search** at
  solve time to stitch full solutions. Learned heuristic + search is the whole trick.
- **The reverse-scramble curriculum carried the run.** Starting one move from the
  goal and ramping k outward as each depth cleared 90%, the curriculum advanced
  k=1→11 on its own. You can watch it work: 100% locks in at shallow depths, then
  the frontier moves outward.
- **Where it slows is the lesson, not a bug.** The climb visibly decelerated at
  k=9–11 (100% → 91% → 75%). Each extra move of scramble multiplies the reachable
  state space, so deeper depths need disproportionately more iterations. That
  deceleration *is* the sparse-reward difficulty made visible.

## Limits hit
- 5 minutes / 1500 iterations is enough to master shallow-to-mid scrambles but not
  to push to the deepest depths (k=12–14, up toward God's-number territory of 20).
- 75% at k=11 is below the 90% promotion bar — it was still learning that depth
  when we stopped.

## Honest next steps

  speed — the 3×3 is best-effort by design.
